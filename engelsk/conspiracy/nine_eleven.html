9/11 Conspiracy Theories

On September 11, 2001, 19 militants associated with the Islamic extremist group al-Qaeda conducted a comprehensive terrorist attack against the United States of America, setting of The War on Terror. Approximately 3,000 people died that day, which makes it the largest attack on American soil in history.

The conspiracy theories about 9/11 were among the first disinformation to benefit from the open Internet environment, which allowed them to develop and spread faster than ever before.

11 September 2001: The conspiracy theories still spreading after 20 years

On September 10th, 2021, Shayan Sardarizadeh, a journalist who specializes in investigating online disinformation, conspiracy theories, cults and extremism for BBC Monitoring and BBC World Service, published an article about conspiracy theories on the 9/11 attack: "11 September 2001: conspiracy theories and how they are still spreading".

The first 9/11 conspiracy theories appeared on the internet just hours after the attacks, on 11 September 2001, and with the rise of social media, have grown in scope and scale ever since.

Extensive reports by the 9/11 Commission, US government agencies and expert groups have refuted the existence of any hidden conspiracy, but activist groups in the US, like the 9/11 Truth movement, say the facts have been hidden. Leading members of the movement have also embraced conspiracies about Covid-19 and vaccines.

One of the claims widely shared online is, “Jet fuel cannot melt steel beams,” suggests that the World Trade Center’s Twin Towers were demolished by explosive. Also, the collapse of 7 World Trade Center, a 47-story skyscraper in the near vicinity of the Twin Towers, has fueled many conspiracy theories questioning the collapse, which happened hours after the Twin Towers without being hit by a plane or directly targeted.

Another theory falsely claims no Jewish people were killed in the attach because 4,000 Jewish employees at the World Trade Center had received advance notice not to turnup for work.
