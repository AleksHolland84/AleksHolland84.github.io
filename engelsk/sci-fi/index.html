You are about to go on a journey through the futuristic sci fi genre. You will read an extract of Douglas Adams’ bestselling book, read a part of a Japanese cyberpunk manga, and watch one of many films in the sci fi genre. Throughout the course you’ll learn about some of the unique and classic artifacts (films, books, and graphic novels) that is part of the sci fi genre.

When you are done with this topic, you:

  • will know what sci fi is
  • will have read an extract of a sci fi novel
  • will have watched a sci fi move (of cause loving it!)
  • will have created your own sci fi universe

    Intro to Sci Fi

    Science fiction, also often known as 'sci-fi', is a genre of literature that is imaginative and based around science. It relies heavily on scientific facts, theories, and principles as support for its settings, characters, themes, and plot.

    Ghost in the Shell

    In the near future, Major is the first of her kind: a human who is cyber-enhanced to be a perfect soldier devoted to stopping the world's most dangerous criminals. When terrorism reaches a new level that includes the ability to hack into people's minds and control them, Major is uniquely qualified to stop it.

    The Hitchhicker's Guide to the Galaxy

    Seconds before the Earth is demolished to make way for a galactic freeway, Arthur Dent is plucked off the planet by his friend Ford Prefect, a researcher for the revised edition of the The Hitch Hiker's Guide to the Galaxy who, for the last fifteen years, has been posing as an out of work actor.

    I dette forløb arbejder eleverne med science fiction-genren.

    Forløbet tager udgangspunkt i et udvalg af klassiske materialer med henblik på at præsentere eleverne for sci fi genren igennem forskellige kulturteknikker.

    Materialerne, der indgår i forløbet, er udvalgt med henblik på at inspirere og fastholde elevernes nysgerrighed, og har til hensigt at imødekomme både de verbalt, auditivt og visuelt orienterede elever.
