Moon Landing

It took 400,000 NASA employees and contractors to put Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin on the moon in 1969 – but only one man to spread the idea that it was all a hoax. His name was Bill Kaysing.

www.theguardian.com

There were six successful manned missions to the moon, all within the Apollo program. In July 1969, Apollo 11 astronauts Niel Armstrong and Edwin “Buzz” Aldrin walked on the moon.

That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind - Niel Armstrong, At 10:56 p.m. ET on July 20, 1969,

In June 1976, Bill Kaysing published his book, “We Never Went to the Moon: America’s Thirty Billion Doller Swindel!”. Bill Kaysing was an American writer and is considered to be the founder of the moon landing conspiracy theories. Throughout his book, Kaysing catalogues inconsistencies that “prove” the Apollo landings were fake. On page 73, in his book, he uses these inconsistencies to concludes that, THE TRIP TO THE MOON WAS A HOAX.
