The Troubles

The Troubles, violent sectarian conflict from about 1968 to 1998 in Northern Ireland between the overwhelmingly Protestant unionists (loyalists), who desired the province to remain part of the United Kingdom, and the overwhelmingly Roman Catholic nationalists (republicans), who wanted Northern Ireland to become part of the republic of Ireland. What You Need to Know About The Troubles

The Troubles is a term used to describe a period of conflict in Northern Ireland that lasted about 30 years, from the late 1960s until the Good Friday Agreement in 1998.

However, the origins of the Troubles can be traced back hundreds of years. The Plantation of Ulster at the beginning of the 17th century settled Protestants from lowland Scotland and the North of England causing a sectarian split in the population of the province; something that still resonates today and fed into the Troubles.

Catholics predominantly consider themselves Irish and hold nationalist views - they want an independent Ireland free from British control. Protestants identify largely as British and unionist, meaning they wish to remain linked to the United Kingdom.

Task for Belfast

Introduction and purpose

The purpose of these exercises is to interpret and analyse 'Belfast' and discuss the themes of the film. You will get to practice your spoken and written English.


Trailer

Tasks

In pairs

  • Watch the trailer for 'Belfast' (see above). What is the atmosphere of the trailer – which emotions are the trailer trying to convey to the audience? What are the main conflicts presented in the trailer?

Individually

  • Write a summary of the story in 'Belfast'. Your summary must be approximately 250 words and it must tell its reader the story from the beginning to the end of the film. Your summary must be divided into at least three paragraphs and have three headings: ‘Conflict’, ‘Love’, and ‘Leaving Belfast’.

In pairs

  • Discuss the themes of the film. Partner up and take turns interviewing each other about the themes and conflicts of the film:
    • Religion
      • What is the role of religion in the film?
      • What does Buddy think about the differences between Protestants and Catholics?
      • What do you think the film wants to tell us about religion?
    • Family
      • Try to describe the members of Buddy's family. What kind of life are they living?
      • How are they helping each other?
      • What are their problems?
    • Love
      • Try to describe Buddy's love interest. Is it normal for a nine-year-old boy to think about love and marriage?
      • How is love affecting Buddy's actions in the film, both in school and in his thoughts about Protestants and Catholics?
    • Childhood
      • How is Buddy's childhood portrayed in 'Belfast'?
      • What are the most important things for him? Is it a good childhood? Why/why not?
    • Civil war in Northern Ireland
      • What does the film tell the audience about the conflict in Northern Ireland?
      • Did you know about the conflict before you saw the film?
      • Did you learn new things about the conflict?

Individually

  • Write a character analysis (250 words) of Buddy. Use two of the pictures from the film in your character analysis.
  • Write about your own opinion on the film. Try to explain what the film attempts to achieve. Is it successful? Why/why not?
  • Write a short story about one of the persons in the picture below. Your story must be written with a first-person narrator who is one of the persons in the picture.
